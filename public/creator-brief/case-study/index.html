The Breakdown
Problem At Backal Hospitality Group, I built the creator affiliate program from scratch across eight brands. One of the most time-consuming parts was writing briefs. The format needed to be consistent enough to scale while flexible enough to adapt to very different brands, audiences, and campaign objectives. And every time you iterate by hand, the briefs start drifting from the original strategy. Small changes compound, files pile up, and version control becomes its own job.
Constraint Eight brands, each with a distinct identity and audience. Every brief had to feel tailored to the specific venue and campaign, but the underlying structure had to be repeatable enough that someone other than me could pick it up and run with it.
Solution A tool that packages that pattern recognition into a system. Input a brand, a campaign objective, an audience, a platform, and a few constraints. It generates a structured creator partnership brief: recommended creator archetypes with follower ranges and content style, creative direction with do's and don'ts, specific content deliverables with format and talking points, a timeline with milestones, and a measurement framework tied to the original objective.

The Takeaway

The value of a creator brief is the structure underneath it: knowing which creator archetypes match which objectives, what deliverables actually drive results on which platforms, and how to tie measurement back to the original goal. This tool proves that structure can be systematized, scaled, and handed to anyone running a creator program.
