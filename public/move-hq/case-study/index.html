App

MoveHQ

Life management app built to coordinate a cross-country move. Task tracking, apartment hunting, budgets, and vibe checks.

React Full-Stack App Product Design
The Breakdown
Problem Planning a cross-country move to New York is six or seven projects running in parallel on different timelines: apartment hunting, budget tracking, job searching, neighborhood research, packing logistics, utility transfers, lease overlaps. Every existing tool I found addressed one of those in isolation. Nothing handled the fact that they all affect each other simultaneously.
Constraint Two people coordinating the move meant the system had to support shared visibility without overcomplicating things. I needed something that showed the full picture at a glance. A single dashboard, budget health, task progress, apartment comparisons, and emotional state, without eighteen linked Notion databases to get there.
Solution A full-stack life management app with nine views: a dashboard with stat cards and next actions, a budget tracker with category breakdowns and progress bars, a payout planner with interactive allocation sliders, a listings kanban for apartment hunting, a task manager with area filtering and priority tagging, a decision log, a paperwork tracker, a vibe check (dual mood sliders with a 30-day trend chart), and a settings panel. Pre-populated with realistic sample data so it reads as a tool in active use.

The Takeaway

This was the first thing I built with AI development tools. It taught me that coding doesn't have to be a bottleneck for my ideas. I just needed something that could help me synthesize what to track, what to surface, what to hide, how to structure information so it's useful under stress. The tool is a product design project more than a technical one, and it works because the decisions behind it came from real, lived urgency.
