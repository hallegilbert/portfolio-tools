Field dispatches / July 2025
Day One / Friday
July 18, 2025
Arrived with nothing to prove.
First sighting at noon, walking through the gate at Big Mountain Ranch. Sunglasses. No companion. Walked in like she'd been here before.

Spotted side-stage during Red Clay Strays, writing in a notebook. Sat alone on the hill above the Great Northern Stage during Shane Smith & the Saints.

By 11pm she was at The Great Northern on Central Ave, two whiskeys in, talking to some guy in a Corb Lund shirt about something that made her laugh hard enough to almost fall off the stool.
Confirmed: 1:47am, walking north on Central Ave. Humming something nobody could place.
Today's hat: "COWGIRL DOWN". Dusty cream corduroy, rust embroidery. The one that started it.
Day Two / Saturday
July 19, 2025
Rode the bull at Blue Moon. Didn't flinch.
Changed hats. This one said NO MONEY / RICH. Someone asked where she got it. She said "I made it" and walked away.

Spotted at the rodeo ring mid-afternoon, boots crossed on the fence. A man walked up. Leather jacket, too much confidence, the kind that means trouble or history. They talked for four minutes. He touched her arm. She moved it. He left first.

She watched Mumford & Sons from the same spot and didn't move for ninety minutes.

By midnight she was at Blue Moon off Montana 40. Nightclub and rodeo in one building. She got on the mechanical bull. Eleven seconds. Got off, tipped her hat to nobody, ordered another drink.

A flyer turned up in the women's restroom. COWGIRL DOWN. HAVE YOU SEEN HER? QR code. Nothing else.
Confirmed: 2:12am, Casey's rooftop, alone at the rail, looking at the mountains.
Today's hat: "NO MONEY / RICH". Faded denim corduroy, cream thread. For girls who tip well on an empty bank account.
Day Three / Sunday
July 20, 2025
Got onstage at an afterparty. Nobody asked her to.
New hat. Pink thread on cream: I ♥ SWEET MEN. A woman at Spotted Bear Spirits asked to buy it off her head. She said no. "This one's personal."

Tyler Childers closed the festival. She was near the front, eyes closed for half the set. The leather jacket guy was three rows back. She knew. Didn't turn around.

After the last song she went straight to The Remington. Afterparty band playing covers. She ended up onstage with a tambourine for one song. Nobody's sure how. Stepped down, half-bowed, slipped out the side door.

Last flyer found stapled to a telephone pole on Spokane Ave around 3am. It just said: LAST SEEN WESTBOUND.
Final sighting: 3:22am, walking south on Railway Street toward the Amtrak station. One hat on, two in hand. Gone by morning.
Today's hat: "I ♥ SWEET MEN". Cream corduroy, pink thread. The one she said was personal.
